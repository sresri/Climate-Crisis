Analyzing Cause and Effect of Humans on Climate Change

The human-caused climate crisis has become an increasingly concerning problem over the years. What are the causes of climate change, and how does it affect the environment? To emphasize the causes and effects of the crisis, we analyzed one specific cause, and one specific effect: Deforestation in the Amazon Rainforest, and the drying of Lake Mead. We designed a clever program to analyze the color of pixels of a satellite image of the Amazon Rainforest to discern the ratio of the green pixels (i.e. forested area) to brown pixels (i.e. deforested area). This will help show the progression of deforestation over a span of 20 years, from 2000-2020. The algorithm developed was so eloquent and versatile that we were able to apply it to Lake Mead with little to no change, showcasing how one can easily compare different images and realize the effects humans are having on our environment.

Simple ways to protect the Amazon rainforest (and the environment!)

Reduce the amount of wood and paper used:
  • Trees from this forest are logged to produce the paper and wood that you buy. By decreasing the amount you use, through simple measures like saving paper, you can indirectly reduce the amount of trees logged. Saving paper can be done in very simple ways. For example:
    1. Fill in all the pages of a notebook before getting a new one
    2. Use both sides of paper
    3. Try to buy recycled paper
Confront Companies:
  • If you feel a company is environmentally harmful, then contact the company, expressing your concern.
  • Another option is the boycott the company, or refusing to use its services/products.
  • Confronting companies directly is a good option since it reminds them that the general public will hold them accountable for environmental destruction.
